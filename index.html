Top 10 boba tea facts

  1. Tapioca pearls are made from cassava — a starchy root — and they get their chewy texture from being cooked and sweetened.
  2. Bubble tea (also called boba) was popularized in Taiwan in the 1980s and has since spread worldwide with tons of local twists.
  3. Most bubble tea shops let you customize sweetness and ice level, it’s okay to ask for "less sugar" if you want it milder.
  4. There are many bases: black or green tea, milk tea, fruit tea, and even coffee or yogurt versions, try different ones to see what you like.
  5. Not all toppings are tapioca: there are popping boba (fruit juice centers), jelly, pudding, and more, texture is a big part of the fun.
  6. Classic milk tea gets its creaminess from condensed milk or dairy; there are excellent plant-based alternatives like oat or almond milk.
  7. Freshly cooked pearls are soft on the outside and chewy inside; leftovers can harden, so they’re best eaten soon after preparation.
  8. Flavors range from simple (classic milk, jasmine) to bold (brown sugar, taro, matcha) — brown sugar boba is a popular modern favorite.
  9. When ordering, a wide straw is normal, it’s made that way so you can sip pearls along with the drink.
  10. Boba culture is social: many shops are cozy hangouts, and regional variations mean discovering new takes if you travel.
